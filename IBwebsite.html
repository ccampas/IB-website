The IB Diploma Programme (DP) is a rigorous, academically challenging and balanced programme of education designed to prepare students aged 16 to 19 for success at university and life beyond. The DP aims to encourage students to be knowledgeable, inquiring, caring and compassionate, and to develop intercultural understanding, open-mindedness and the attitudes necessary to respect and evalu- ate a range of viewpoints. Approaches to teaching and learning (ATL) within the DP are deliberate strategies, skills and attitudes that permeate the teaching and learning environment. In the DP students develop skills from ve ATL categories: thinking, research, social, self-management and communication.

To ensure both breadth and depth of knowledge and understanding, students must choose at least one subject from ve groups: 1) their best language, 2) additional lan- guage(s), 3) social sciences, 4) experimental sciences, and 5) mathematics. Students may choose either an arts subject from group 6, or a second subject from groups 1 to 5. At least three and not more than four subjects are taken at higher level (240 recommended teaching hours), while the remaining are taken at standard level (150 recommended teaching hours).

In addition, three core elements, the extended essay, theory of knowledge and creativity, action, service, are compulsory and central to the philosophy of the programme.

Program Requirements
  • The IB requires a 28 or higher score to pass
  • They also ask that you complete a certain amount of outcomes in CAS (Community, Action and Service)
  • You have not been judged of malpractice
  • As far as individual grades go:
    • You have to get higher than a 2/7 for all the High Level classes
    • You have to get higher than a 1/7 for all the Standard Level courses
    • You can't have a 2 in more than 3 classes
    • Have at least 12 points in your Higher Level Subjects
    • Have at least 19 points in your Standard Level Subjects
    • Grades A-E have been awarded for both the Extended Essay and Theory of Knowladge, with at least a D in one of them

IB learners profile

  • Inquirer: nurture our curiosity, develope skills for inquiry and research. Know how to learn with enthusiasm and sustain our love of learning through life.
  • Knowladgable: Develope and use conceptual undestanding, exploring knowlede across a range of disciplines. Engage with issues and ideas that have local and global signigicance.
  • Thinkers: Use critical and creative thinking skills to analuse and take responsible action on coplex problems. Exercise initiative in making reasoned, and ethical decisions.
  • Communicators: Express ourselves confidently and creativly in more than one language in many ways. Collaborate effectively, listening carfully to the prespectives of other individual groups.
  • Principled: We act with integrity and honesty, with a strong sense of fairness and justice, and with respect for the dignity and rights of the people everywhere. We take responsibility for our actions and their consequences.
  • Open-Minded: Critically appreciate our own cultures and personal histories, as well as values and traditions of others, and we are willing to grow from their experience.
  • Caring:We show empath, compassion and respect. We have a commitment to service and we act to make a positive difference in the lives of others and in the world around us.
  • Risk-Takers:We approach uncertainty with forethought deremination; we work independently and cooperatively to explore new ideas and innovative strategies. We are resourceful and resilient in the face of challenges and change.
  • Balanced: We understand the importance of balancing the different aspects of our lives (intellectual, physical, and emotional) to achieve well-being for ourselves and others. We recognize our indpendence with other people and with the world in which we live.
  • Reflective: We consider the world and our own ideas and experice. We work to understand our strengths and weaknesses in order to support our learning and personal developement.
